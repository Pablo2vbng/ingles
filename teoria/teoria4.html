Grammar: Obligation, Prohibition, and Permission

1. Obligation, Prohibition, and Permission

Los verbos de obligación, prohibición y permiso (como must, have to, can't, can) se colocan antes del verbo principal.

  • Employees must adhere to the designated dress code.
  • All staff members have to attend mandatory training sessions.
  • Employees can't use company email for personal communication.
  • Employees can use company email for professional communication.

Obligation and Necessity

Usa must, have to, need to para hablar de obligaciones y necesidades.

  • All employees must submit their expense reports by the end of each month.
  • Remote workers have to attend the virtual training sessions.
  • Anyone who identifies a safety hazard needs to report it to their supervisor.

No Obligation

Usa don't have to, don't need to cuando no hay obligación o necesidad de hacer algo.

  • Employees don't have to bring their own devices to the online conference.
  • With flexitime, no one needs to start or end at a fixed time of day.

Prohibition

Usa mustn't, can't, (be) not allowed to para prohibiciones.

  • During meetings, employees mustn't interrupt their colleagues.
  • Staff members can't disclose confidential information.
  • Employees are not allowed to use personal electronic devices during working hours.

Permission

Usa can, (be) allowed to para hablar y pedir permiso.

  • Employees can use personal electronic devices during breaks only.
  • You're allowed to bring your mobile phone into the meeting.
  • Am I allowed to log in to the server from home?

Notas Adicionales

Pasado: Must no tiene forma pasada. Usa had to en su lugar.

  • They must sign in at reception (presente).
  • He had to show his passport at the security desk (pasado).

Preguntas: Para preguntar sobre reglas, usa Do I have to...? o Do I need to...?. Es raro usar must en preguntas sobre reglas.

  • Does he have to lock up every day?
  • Do I need to ask for permission to leave early?

2. Practice

Ejercicio 2.2: Completa los huecos

Completa los espacios con los modales apropiados: must, have to, can, can’t, are not allowed to, mustn't.

  1. Employees submit their weekly reports by Friday at 5:00 p.m.
  2. You use personal social media accounts during work hours.
  3. Remote workers attend the virtual training session scheduled for tomorrow.
  4. Staff members disclose confidential information to external parties.
  5. Do employees bring their own devices to the online conference?
  6. Participants mute their microphones when not speaking in the virtual meeting.
  7. With the new policy, employees work flexible hours as long as they complete their tasks.
  8. You share your login credentials with anyone, even if they are a colleague.
  9. All team members log out of the company’s network at the end of the workday.
  10. Freelancers meet project deadlines to maintain a good working relationship.
