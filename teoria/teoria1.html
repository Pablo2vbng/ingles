Teoría Interactiva: Vocabulario de Trabajo

1. How to Show Interest in a Conversation

En el entorno profesional, mostrar interés durante una conversación es clave. A esto se le llama "active listening".

Frases útiles para mostrar interés:

  • Reaccionar: That's interesting!, Really?, Oh, cool!
  • Acordar: Right., I see., Sounds good.
  • Reaccionar a buenas noticias: That sounds great!, That's fantastic!

Ejercicio 1: Completa el diálogo

Elige la mejor opción del menú desplegable para completar la conversación.

Mark: I'm organizing our next big product launch. It's for a new app.

Anna (1): I work in app development.

Mark: We are planning to launch it in Europe next month.

Anna (2): What does the app do exactly?

Mark: It helps people learn new languages. We are getting very positive feedback.

Anna (3): Maybe we can collaborate sometime.

2. Grammar: Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

El Present Simple se usa para rutinas y hechos. El Present Continuous se usa para acciones en progreso o situaciones temporales.

Ejercicio 2: Elige la forma correcta

Escribe la forma correcta del verbo (Present Simple o Continuous). Después, pulsa el botón para corregir.

  1. She normally (work) in the office, but this week she (work) from home.
  2. My team (usually / hold) meetings on Mondays.
  3. Please be quiet, I (talk) to a client on the phone.
  4. The company (want) to expand its services to Asia.
  5. Right now, the marketing team (develop) a new strategy.

3. Pronunciation: Word Stress

El "word stress" es la sílaba que se pronuncia con más fuerza en una palabra. Acertar con el "word stress" es clave para que te entiendan bien.
